The Deg-Bhapka Method

Copper, water,
fire, and patience.

Every attar in our house begins the same way it began four centuries ago. A copper still buried in clay. A bamboo pipe. A receiver of pure sandalwood oil. And the slowest possible fire — because hurry destroys scent.

01
Harvest at dawn

Roses picked before sunrise — the petals still hold the night's water, and the scent has not been burned off by sun.

02
Into the deg

Flowers and water in the copper still. Sealed with a dough of wheat flour. Heated over a slow cow-dung fire for hours.

03
Through the bamboo

The fragrant steam travels through bamboo — never metal — to keep the scent pure. It then enters the bhapka receiver.

04
Caught by sandalwood

The bhapka holds pure sandalwood oil. The flower's essence dissolves into the wood. After 15-20 such cycles, the attar is ready.
